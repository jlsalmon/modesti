MODESTI

Monitoring Data Entry System for Technical Infrastructure

What is MODESTI?

MODESTI is a tool for centrally managing the definition of monitoring data circulating at CERN. There are tens of thousands of sensors and other pieces of equipment at CERN that are constantly emitting data (referred to as data points). Those data points need to be constantly monitored to make sure that they are healthy.

There are multiple systems at CERN that are used to acquire these data points, and each has a different way of representing that data. MODESTI is aware of these systems, and is capable of creating, updating and deleting data points on them. It also makes sure that the relevant people are informed when points are added or updated, keeping those who need to know in the loop. When everybody has agreed that the data points are correct, they are automatically configured on the target system and are ready to be used in operation.

Alarms

Data points from some equipment are so critical that they trigger an alarm if their value goes above or below a certain threshold, or if they stop being sent altogether. It is absolutely essential that these data points are well-defined and that everybody knows what to do when that alarm is triggered.

The operational staff working in the CERN Control Centre are responsible for monitoring and acting upon alarms, which are centrally gathered and displayed in LASER, the main alarm system.

Therefore, when MODESTI detects that an alarm is being created, the operators are automatically informed and must explicitly review and agree upon the format of the alarm. Once agreed upon, the alarm definition is given to LASER so that it is able to display the alarm when it is received.

Why do I need MODESTI?

Equipment is added and removed every day, so there needs to be a simple and easy way to centrally record what data is being emitted from that equipment. MODESTI allows you to define data points for a wide range of systems, and can easily integrate new systems thanks to its flexible plugin-based architecture.
